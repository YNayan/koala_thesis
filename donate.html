Why US?

Approximately 7.6 million companion pets are surrendered to a shelter every year; 4.2 million are dogs and 1.4 million dogs are euthanized. Animals have no voice of their own, no social safety net and very few rights under the law. They are vulnerable and, unfortunately, mistreated more often than most of us would like to think. Shelters and rescue operations for pets need donations from the community to operate. Many animal shelters do not have the resources they need to care for sick or injured animals. Approximately 80 percent of animals that are euthanized are healthy or treatable and could have been adopted into new homes. Senior dogs and bully breeds are also high on the list of dogs that are at-risk for euthanasia.
